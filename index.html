Got Questions?

EWS1 ‚Äî Everything You Need to Know

An EWS1 (External Wall System 1) form is a standardised document introduced by RICS, UK Finance and the Building Societies Association in December 2019. It certifies that the external wall system of a residential building has been assessed for fire safety. Mortgage lenders require it to approve loans on flats in buildings where cladding or other combustible materials may be present. Without it, selling or remortgaging a flat can be extremely difficult.

Since January 2023, lenders may only require an EWS1 form for buildings over 18 metres (approx. 6 storeys) in height. However, individual lenders retain discretion and some still request EWS1 forms for lower-rise buildings if there is reason to believe combustible cladding is present. If you are trying to sell or remortgage a flat and a lender has requested an EWS1 form, you will need one regardless of building height.

A1 means no combustible materials are present in the external wall ‚Äî no further action required. A2 means combustible materials are present but the fire risk is sufficiently low that no remediation is needed. B1 means combustible materials are present, but mitigating features (such as sprinklers) mean the risk is acceptable. B2 is the most serious ‚Äî remediation is required before the building is considered safe and a mortgage can usually be obtained. The vast majority of buildings assessed receive an A2 or B1 rating.

Our typical turnaround is 14 days from the site survey to issuing the completed EWS1 form. The site survey itself usually takes half a day to a full day depending on building size and complexity. In urgent cases (such as an imminent property sale), we offer expedited 7-day service ‚Äî please contact us to discuss your requirements.

Typically, the freeholder or building owner is responsible for commissioning and paying for the EWS1 assessment. Under the Building Safety Act 2022, qualifying leaseholders (those who owned the property before February 2022) are broadly protected from being charged for cladding-related assessments and remediation. Individual sellers occasionally commission private EWS1 assessments to facilitate a property sale, though this is less common. Contact us for guidance specific to your situation.

Our EWS1 assessments start from ¬£950 + VAT for smaller buildings and increase with the complexity and size of the building. Larger or more complex buildings requiring invasive investigations or laboratory sample analysis will cost more. We always provide a fixed-price quote before commencing work so there are no surprises. Contact us for a bespoke quote for your building.

A B2 rating means the external wall system poses an unacceptable fire risk and remediation work is required. This does not mean the building is immediately unsafe to occupy ‚Äî the fire risk is assessed in the context of the building's height, existing mitigations and the nature of the materials. Our remediation consultancy team can guide you through the next steps: obtaining a technical specification, tendering for contractors, applying for Building Safety Fund grants, and liaising with the Building Safety Regulator.
